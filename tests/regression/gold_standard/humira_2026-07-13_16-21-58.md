### Chronic Recurrent Multifocal Osteomyelitis

#### Literature — Chronic Recurrent Multifocal Osteomyelitis

**Evidence strength:** weak, supports
**Relevant studies:** 1

A single case report (PMID: 33087018) describes a 25-year-old woman with adult-onset chronic non-bacterial osteomyelitis (CNO) of the mandible who was treated with adalimumab after conventional therapies (antibiotics, NSAIDs, glucocorticoids, and cortical osteotomy) failed; pain and swelling resolved promptly and CT/MRI showed improvement in osteolytic and sclerotic bone lesions at one and two years. The remaining abstracts were contaminated because they studied different diseases (rheumatoid arthritis, Crohn's disease) or different drugs (infliximab, unspecified anti-TNF agents) and do not constitute direct evidence for adalimumab in chronic recurrent multifocal osteomyelitis. The available evidence supports the potential utility of adalimumab for CNO/CRMO but is limited to a single case report.

**Key findings:**
- A 25-year-old woman with CNO of the mandible refractory to antibiotics, NSAIDs, glucocorticoids, and cortical osteotomy experienced immediate resolution of pain and swelling after initiation of adalimumab (PMID: 33087018).
- CT and MRI imaging confirmed improvement in osteolytic and sclerotic bone lesions and osteomyelitis at both one and two years after adalimumab treatment (PMID: 33087018).
- The authors concluded that anti-TNF-α antibody (adalimumab) may be an effective therapy for CNO resistant to conventional treatment (PMID: 33087018).

### Asthma

#### Literature — Asthma

**Evidence strength:** weak, supports
**Relevant studies:** 1

The only relevant evidence for adalimumab in asthma comes from a single murine model study (PMID: 24882395), which found that adalimumab significantly reduced lung inflammation, cellular infiltration, peribronchial smooth muscle hypertrophy, and alveolar wall thickness in OVA-induced asthma. The remaining abstracts were excluded as contaminated: PMID 33569770 and 33150590 concern azithromycin (a different drug), PMID 16085220 concerns etanercept (a different drug in the same class), and PMID 30176222 concerns TNF-α genetic polymorphisms with no therapeutic intervention. The evidence from the single animal study supports the biological plausibility of adalimumab for asthma but is limited to a preclinical model.

**Key findings:**
- Adalimumab significantly reduced the severity of lung damage compared to untreated OVA-induced asthma rats, including reduced inflammatory cells around bronchi and bronchioles (PMID: 24882395).
- Adalimumab reduced alveolar wall inflammation and alveolar wall thickness in a murine model of acute asthma (PMID: 24882395).
- Peribronchial smooth muscle hypertrophy and oedema were significantly reduced after adalimumab administration in OVA-sensitized rats (PMID: 24882395).

**Supporting PMIDs:** [24882395](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24882395/)

#### Clinical Trials

**Development stage:** Early-phase only, no completed pivotal readout
Clinical investigation of adalimumab in refractory asthma has been limited to early-phase work. One relevant study is on record: NCT00512863, a Phase 2 dose-finding trial titled 'A Dose-Finding Study to Evaluate the Safety, Efficacy and Pharmacokinetics of Adalimumab in Subjects With Refractory Asthma,' which was withdrawn before enrolling participants. No active trials in this indication are currently on record.

### Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus

#### Literature — Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus

**Evidence strength:** class-level signal (no direct evidence for this drug)
**Relevant studies:** 3

The retrieved abstracts do not contain direct clinical or preclinical evidence for adalimumab specifically in type 1 diabetes mellitus. PMID 25053869 studied infliximab (a different TNF-α inhibitor) and TNF-R1 blocking agents — not adalimumab — on monocyte apoptosis in T1DM patients, making it contaminated. PMID 33207093 studied golimumab (another different TNF-α inhibitor) in youth with new-onset T1DM, also contaminated. The remaining relevant abstracts (PMIDs 38531504, 28426801, 41349385) provide class-level biological rationale: TNF-α is significantly elevated in T1DM patients (PMID 28426801), TNF-α promoter variants G-308A and C-857T are linked to increased T1DM risk (PMID 41349385), and a meta-analysis found that TNF-α inhibitors as a class (not adalimumab specifically) were among the drivers of preserved 4-hour C-peptide AUC in T1DM immunotherapy trials (PMID 38531504). No direct evidence for adalimumab in T1DM was identified.

**Key findings:**
- A meta-analysis of 23 studies (1631 T1DM cases, 1429 controls) found significantly elevated serum TNF-α levels in T1DM patients across all age, disease duration, and ethnicity subgroups (PMID: 28426801).
- TNF-α promoter variants G-308A and C-857T were associated with increased risk of type 1 diabetes in a meta-analysis of 24 case-control studies, confirmed by trial sequential analysis (PMID: 41349385).
- A systematic review and meta-analysis of 34 clinical trials found that TNF-α inhibitors (as a class) were among the main drivers of preserved 4-hour C-peptide AUC in T1DM patients treated with nonantigen-based immunotherapies, and were associated with reduced daily insulin dosage without increased hypoglycemia risk (PMID: 38531504).

**Supporting PMIDs:** [38531504](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38531504/)

**Context (non-efficacy) PMIDs:** [28426801](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28426801/), [41349385](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41349385/)

#### Clinical Trials

**Development stage:** Early-phase only, no completed pivotal readout
Two Phase 1/2 trials have been identified for adalimumab in new-onset Type 1 diabetes. NCT07061574 is an active, recruiting randomized Phase 1/2 study evaluating low-dose anti-thymocyte globulin (ATG) followed by either adalimumab or verapamil in patients with new-onset Type 1 diabetes. A second Phase 1/2 study, NCT02586831 (Diabetes Islet Preservation Immune Treatment), was withdrawn prior to enrolling any participants due to funding issues and did not generate efficacy or safety data.
