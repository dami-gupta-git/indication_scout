## Findings by Disease

### Raynaud Disease

#### Literature — Raynaud Disease

**Evidence strength:** strong, mixed
**Relevant studies:** 4

Multiple randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials have evaluated sildenafil for Raynaud's phenomenon (RP). Two trials found significant benefits: modified-release sildenafil reduced RP attack frequency by 44% versus 18.1% for placebo in patients with limited cutaneous systemic sclerosis (PMID: 21360507), and sildenafil 100 mg/day improved digital blood flow and RP symptom duration in systemic sclerosis patients (PMID: 28281457). However, an n-of-1 trial series found that while sildenafil was statistically superior to placebo, the aggregated effect size was not clinically relevant and substantial heterogeneity in patient response was observed (PMID: 30383134), and a mechanistic study found that sildenafil increases sympathetically mediated vascular tone via baroreflex activation, which may partially offset its vasodilatory effects (PMID: 23443723).

**Key findings:**
- Modified-release sildenafil reduced RP attack frequency by a mean of 44.0% versus 18.1% for placebo (P=0.034) in patients with RP secondary to limited cutaneous systemic sclerosis (PMID: 21360507)
- Sildenafil 100 mg/day for 8 weeks significantly improved finger blood flow before and after cold stimulus (p=0.026 and p=0.028, respectively) compared to placebo in systemic sclerosis patients (PMID: 28281457)
- Sildenafil significantly improved duration of RP attacks and RP VAS score compared to placebo in systemic sclerosis patients (PMID: 28281457)
- In an n-of-1 trial series, the probability that sildenafil at 40 mg or 80 mg was more effective than placebo exceeded 90% for most outcomes, but the aggregated effect size was not clinically relevant (PMID: 30383134)
- Substantial heterogeneity in sildenafil's efficacy was observed among participants in the n-of-1 trial series, with clinically relevant efficacy in some patients but not others (PMID: 30383134)
- Sildenafil significantly increased sympathetically mediated vascular tone and plasma norepinephrine levels compared to placebo in healthy men, suggesting alpha-adrenergic vasoconstriction may partially offset its vasodilatory effects (PMID: 23443723)
- The percentage reduction in forearm vascular resistance during phentolamine was significantly lower after sildenafil than placebo (-63% ± 3% vs -73% ± 3%; P=0.0002), indicating increased sympathetic vascular tone with sildenafil (PMID: 23443723)

**Supporting PMIDs:** [21360507](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21360507/), [28281457](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28281457/)

**Contradicting PMIDs:** [30383134](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30383134/)

**Context (non-efficacy) PMIDs:** [23443723](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23443723/)

⚠️ **Indication-specific safety:** Multiple abstracts report safety findings for sildenafil in the context of Raynaud's phenomenon, including adverse effects limiting treatment (PMID: [16275885](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16275885/)), adverse effects reducing the risk-benefit profile (PMID: [30383134](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30383134/)), critical finger ischemia and myocardial fibrosis after sudden sildenafil discontinuation in an SSc/RP patient (PMID: [27608801](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27608801/)), safety outcomes in an SSc/RP pilot study (PMID: [22833239](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22833239/)), safety data from a systematic review of RP treatments (PMID: [38306796](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38306796/)), and hypotension as a side effect leading to treatment discontinuation in RP patients (PMID: [33422171](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33422171/)).

### Covid-19

#### Literature — Covid-19

**Evidence strength:** moderate, mixed
**Relevant studies:** 2

Two randomized, placebo-controlled trials evaluated sildenafil specifically in COVID-19 patients. In a double-blind RCT of ICU patients with severe COVID-19 and pulmonary arterial hypertension, sildenafil significantly reduced mortality and intubation risks when adjusted for PAH (OR=0.21 and OR=0.26, respectively) and shortened ICU stay (15 vs. 19 days, p<0.001) (PMID: 37243243). In a pilot RCT of COVID-19 inpatients with perfusion mismatch on sCTA, sildenafil was associated with no requirement for invasive mechanical ventilation versus 4 patients in the placebo arm (p=0.04) and a shorter hospital stay (9 vs. 12 days, p=0.04), though primary oxygenation endpoints were not significantly different (PMID: 34980198). The evidence supports repurposing sildenafil for COVID-19, particularly in patients with pulmonary vascular complications, though both studies were small and call for larger confirmatory trials.

**Key findings:**
- In a double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT of 150 ICU patients with severe COVID-19 and PAH, sildenafil (0.25 mg/kg t.i.d. for one week) significantly reduced mortality risk (OR=0.21, 95% CI: 0.05-0.89) and intubation risk (OR=0.26, 95% CI: 0.08-0.86) when adjusted for PAH (PMID: 37243243).
- Sildenafil significantly shortened ICU stay compared to placebo (15 vs. 19 days, p<0.001) in severe COVID-19 patients with PAH (PMID: 37243243).
- In a pilot triple-blinded RCT of 40 COVID-19 inpatients with perfusion mismatch on sCTA, no patients in the sildenafil arm required invasive mechanical ventilation versus 4 in the placebo arm (log-rank p=0.04) (PMID: 34980198).
- Sildenafil-treated COVID-19 patients with perfusion mismatch had a significantly shorter median hospital stay than placebo (9 IQR 7-12 days vs. 12 IQR 9-21 days, p=0.04) (PMID: 34980198).
- No statistically significant differences in primary oxygenation parameters (PaO2/FiO2 ratio and A-a gradient) were found between sildenafil and placebo groups in COVID-19 patients with perfusion mismatch (PMID: 34980198).

**Supporting PMIDs:** [37243243](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37243243/), [34980198](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34980198/)

**Contradicting PMIDs:** [37243243](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37243243/), [34980198](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34980198/)

⚠️ **Indication-specific safety:** PMID: [35568507](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35568507/) specifically reports on the use of sildenafil in patients with severe COVID-19 pneumonitis, a context in which safety findings (adverse events or harms) would be directly tied to COVID-19 treatment use.
