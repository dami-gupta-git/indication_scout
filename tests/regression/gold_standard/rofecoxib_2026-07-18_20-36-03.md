## Drug Safety

_Drug-wide safety signal — applies to the drug generally, not to any single candidate indication below._

**Safety:** Rofecoxib was withdrawn worldwide in 2004 due to a significantly increased risk of serious cardiovascular events, including myocardial infarction and stroke, a signal confirmed across multiple pharmacovigilance reports and clinical trials (PMID: [15713943](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15713943/), PMID: [15582059](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15582059/), PMID: [15705456](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15705456/)). Its use for pain is therefore contraindicated given this established, fatal cardiovascular harm.

**Safety PMIDs:** [15713943](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15713943/), [15582059](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15582059/), [15705456](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15705456/)

## Findings by Disease

### Pain

#### Literature — Pain

**Evidence strength:** strong, supports
**Relevant studies:** 5

Multiple randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials demonstrate that rofecoxib is effective for treating pain across several contexts. Rofecoxib 50 mg was superior to placebo for post-orthopedic surgery pain (PMID: 11198829), postoperative dental pain (PMID: 11143737, PMID: 10566562), and perioperative pain after spine, breast, and orthopedic surgery (PMID: 16430794), and it significantly attenuated both primary and secondary inflammatory hyperalgesia in a human UV-B pain model (PMID: 15661439). The evidence supports the use of rofecoxib for pain management.

**Key findings:**
- Rofecoxib 50 mg once daily was superior to placebo for all single-dose measures of pain relief in post-orthopedic surgery pain, and reduced supplemental narcotic analgesia use (P=0.005) and pain on global evaluations (P=0.041) over days 2-5 compared to placebo (PMID: 11198829).
- Across six placebo-controlled trials in postoperative dental pain, rofecoxib 50 mg was consistently more effective than placebo for all measures of analgesic efficacy and demonstrated analgesic effect comparable to maximum single analgesic doses of NSAIDs (PMID: 11143737).
- In a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover trial using the UV-B pain model, rofecoxib significantly increased heat pain perception threshold (P<0.0001) and heat pain tolerance threshold (P<0.0001), and reduced the area of secondary hyperalgesia by approximately 15-17% compared to placebo (PMID: 15661439).
- Perioperative administration of rofecoxib 50 mg significantly decreased pain scores (median 0 vs. 1, P significant) and morphine consumption (18 vs. 22.5 mg) compared to placebo after spine, breast, and orthopedic surgery (PMID: 16430794).
- In a randomized, double-blind trial of postoperative dental pain, rofecoxib 50 mg had superior analgesic efficacy compared to celecoxib 200 mg on all measures including total pain relief over 8 hours (TOPAR8: 18.3 vs. 12.5; P<0.001) and duration of effect (>24 vs. 5.1 hours; P<0.001), and comparable efficacy to ibuprofen 400 mg (TOPAR8: 18.3 vs. 17.0; P=0.460) with longer duration (PMID: 10566562).

**Supporting PMIDs:** [11198829](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11198829/), [11143737](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11143737/), [15661439](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15661439/), [16430794](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16430794/), [10566562](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10566562/)

### Arthritis

#### Literature — Arthritis

**Evidence strength:** strong, supports
**Relevant studies:** 5

Multiple randomized controlled trials demonstrate that rofecoxib is effective for treating both rheumatoid arthritis and osteoarthritis. Rofecoxib 25 mg and 50 mg once daily significantly improved ACR core set endpoints compared to placebo in RA patients (PMID: 12369656, PMID: 10566565), and rofecoxib 12.5 mg and 25 mg showed efficacy comparable to diclofenac in OA patients over one year (PMID: 10817549). However, safety concerns exist, including greater rates of edema, blood pressure destabilization (PMID: 11304662), and more adverse events compared to some comparators (PMID: 16484875), which must be weighed against the demonstrated efficacy.

**Key findings:**
- Rofecoxib 25 mg and 50 mg once daily provided significant improvement over placebo in all primary endpoints (patient and investigator global assessments, tender and swollen joint counts) in RA patients, with effects evident at week 2 and sustained for 12 weeks (PMID: 12369656).
- In an 8-week placebo-controlled trial in RA, 43.9% of rofecoxib 25 mg patients and 49.7% of rofecoxib 50 mg patients achieved an ACR20 response, significantly better than placebo (PMID: 10566565).
- Rofecoxib 12.5 mg and 25 mg demonstrated clinically comparable efficacy to diclofenac 150 mg/day across all three primary endpoints in a 1-year randomized trial in OA patients (PMID: 10817549).
- In OA patients, rofecoxib 12.5 mg showed significantly better Patient Global Assessment of Response to Therapy than nabumetone 1000 mg (50.4% vs. 43.3%, P=0.043) and placebo (29.5%, P<0.001) at week 6, though significantly more adverse events occurred with rofecoxib than nabumetone (PMID: 16484875).
- In older hypertensive OA patients, nearly twice as many rofecoxib-treated patients experienced edema compared to celecoxib (9.5% vs. 4.9%, P=0.014), and systolic blood pressure increased significantly in 17% of rofecoxib vs. 11% of celecoxib patients (P=0.032) (PMID: 11304662).
- Five rofecoxib and one nabumetone OA patients had confirmed thrombotic cardiovascular events in a 6-week trial, though the difference was not statistically significant (P=0.123) (PMID: 16484875).

**Supporting PMIDs:** [12369656](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12369656/), [10566565](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10566565/), [16484875](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16484875/), [10817549](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10817549/)

### Inflammation

#### Literature — Inflammation

**Evidence strength:** strong, supports
**Relevant studies:** 5

Multiple clinical trials demonstrate that rofecoxib exerts anti-inflammatory effects across diverse settings. Rofecoxib reduced circulating inflammatory markers (leukocyte counts, TNF-α, IL-6, IL-8) after total knee replacement (PMID: 15265408), decreased C-reactive protein, interleukin-6, and malondialdehyde in patients with acute coronary syndromes (PMID: 16330117), and attenuated primary and secondary hyperalgesia in a UV-B inflammatory pain model (PMID: 15661439). One study reported complex gene expression changes including upregulation of some pro-inflammatory mediators (IL-6, CCL2) alongside anti-inflammatory pathways, indicating the drug's effects on inflammation are not uniformly suppressive (PMID: 17070997). Overall, the evidence supports rofecoxib's utility in treating inflammation.

**Key findings:**
- Preoperative rofecoxib significantly reduced leukocyte and neutrophil counts at 12 h and 24 h post-total knee replacement compared to placebo, and lowered serum TNF-α, IL-6, and IL-8 concentrations at multiple postoperative time points (PMID: 15265408).
- Rofecoxib 25 mg daily for one week significantly reduced C-reactive protein (p=0.003), interleukin-6 (p=0.02), and malondialdehyde (p=0.04) in patients with acute coronary syndromes without ST-segment elevation, while placebo showed no change (PMID: 16330117).
- In the UV-B inflammatory pain model, rofecoxib significantly increased heat pain perception threshold (by 1.55°C at 3 h, p<0.0001) and heat pain tolerance threshold (by 1.74°C at 3 h, p<0.0001), and reduced the area of secondary hyperalgesia by 15.6% at 3 h (p=0.007) and 16.8% at 6 h (p<0.001) compared to placebo (PMID: 15661439).
- Rofecoxib treatment increased gene expression of anti-inflammatory mediators ANXA3, SOD2, SOCS3, and IL1RN compared to placebo in a model of acute inflammatory pain, but also increased pro-inflammatory IL-6 and CCL2 gene expression compared to placebo (PMID: 17070997).
- Rofecoxib 50 mg demonstrated superior analgesic efficacy to celecoxib 200 mg (TOPAR8: 18.3 vs 12.5, p<0.001) and comparable efficacy to ibuprofen 400 mg (TOPAR8: 18.3 vs 17.0, p=0.460) in postoperative dental pain associated with inflammation (PMID: 10566562).

**Supporting PMIDs:** [15265408](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15265408/), [15661439](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15661439/), [10566562](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10566562/), [16330117](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16330117/)
