## Findings by Disease

### Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus

#### Literature — Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus

**Evidence strength:** moderate, supports
**Relevant studies:** 2

Two clinical studies of semaglutide in adults with type 1 diabetes provide evidence supporting its repurposing for this condition. A 26-week double-blind RCT (PMID: 40550013) found that once-weekly semaglutide significantly improved a composite endpoint of time-in-range >70%, time-below-range <4%, and ≥5% weight reduction compared to placebo (36% vs. 0%) in adults with type 1 diabetes and obesity using automated insulin delivery. A post-hoc analysis of a double-blind randomized crossover trial (PMID: 41144928) demonstrated that semaglutide rapidly reduced total daily insulin requirements and carbohydrate intake in type 1 diabetes patients on insulin pump therapy, with manageable hypoglycemia risk when using diabetes technology.

**Key findings:**
- In a 26-week double-blind RCT, 36% of adults with type 1 diabetes and obesity receiving semaglutide achieved the composite primary endpoint (CGM time-in-range >70%, time-below-range <4%, and ≥5% weight reduction) versus 0% in the placebo group (p<0.001) (PMID: 40550013)
- Semaglutide reduced body weight by a least-squares mean of -8.8 kg compared to placebo over 26 weeks in adults with type 1 diabetes and obesity (PMID: 40550013)
- Semaglutide improved CGM time-in-range (70–180 mg/dL) by 8.8 percentage points and reduced HbA1c by 0.3 percentage points versus placebo in adults with type 1 diabetes (PMID: 40550013)
- No diabetic ketoacidosis was reported and severe hypoglycemia events were equal between semaglutide and placebo groups (2 each) in the 26-week type 1 diabetes RCT (PMID: 40550013)
- In a randomized crossover trial post-hoc analysis, daily total and bolus insulin requirements were significantly reduced by day 7 of semaglutide initiation in type 1 diabetes patients on insulin pump therapy, with basal insulin significantly reduced by day 32 (PMID: 41144928)
- By day 77 of semaglutide use in type 1 diabetes pump therapy patients, carbohydrate ratios increased by a median of 4.1%, correction factors by 11.2%, and pre-programmed basal rates decreased by 7.9%, indicating need for pump parameter adjustments (PMID: 41144928)
- Median time spent in hypoglycemia was rarely >4% during follow-up visits in type 1 diabetes patients using semaglutide with diabetes technology (PMID: 41144928)

#### Clinical Trials

**Development stage:** Active Phase 3 development on record for this indication (NCT06082063, NCT06909006, NCT03899402, NCT05819138, NCT06894784, NCT06387199)
Semaglutide has an extensive and active clinical trial program in Type 1 Diabetes spanning multiple phases. Among completed studies, the Phase 2 ADJUNCT Semaglutide Treatment in Type 1 Diabetes trial (NCT05537233) and the completed Phase 2/Phase 3 weekly subcutaneous semaglutide as adjunct to closed-loop therapy study (NCT05205928) have both concluded, providing earlier-phase evidence in this population. A completed Phase 4 study examining novel therapeutics and endothelial dysfunction in T1DM patients (NCT05857085) and a completed Not Applicable-phase obesity treatment trial (NCT05390307) round out the finished work.

The active Phase 3 program is substantial. NCT06082063 is a recruiting Phase 3 trial evaluating a multifactorial intervention to reduce cardiovascular disease in Type 1 Diabetes. NCT05819138, also actively recruiting, is a Phase 3 study of the impacts of semaglutide on cardiovascular outcomes in Type 1 Diabetes. NCT06894784 is a recruiting Phase 3 clinical trial evaluating the effects of semaglutide and empagliflozin combined with automated insulin delivery on diabetes control in adults with Type 1 Diabetes. NCT06909006 is a Phase 3 trial of semaglutide treatment in Type 1 Diabetes that is not yet recruiting. Two Phase 2/Phase 3 trials are also active: NCT03899402 (Triple Therapy in T1DM), which is active but no longer recruiting, and NCT06387199, a recruiting study alleviating carbohydrate counting in Type 1 Diabetes patients using a closed-loop system with weekly subcutaneous semaglutide.

Additional earlier-phase work continues in parallel, including a recruiting Phase 2 trial of semaglutide for diabetic kidney disease in Type 1 Diabetes (NCT05822609), a recruiting Early Phase 1 study of GLP-1Ra impact on metabolic outcomes in Stage 2 T1DM with teplizumab (NCT06338553), a Phase 1/Phase 2 study of semaglutide with degludec basal-bolus insulin in early Type 1 Diabetes (NCT06057077, status unknown), a not-yet-recruiting Phase 2 SHIELD-T1D trial combining Shingrix and GLP-1 agonist for beta-cell preservation in recent-onset Type 1 Diabetes (NCT07614412), and a not-yet-recruiting Phase 2 study of GLP-1 RA for Stage 1 Type 1 Diabetes (NCT07430332).

#### Clinical Trials

**Development stage:** Phase 2 completed for this indication, no Phase 3
Semaglutide has been evaluated across multiple studies in nonalcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD)/metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD). At the early mechanistic level, a completed Phase 1 study (NCT03357380) examined how semaglutide acts on early-stage hepatic fibrosis using liver imaging. Two Phase 2 trials have been completed: NCT04216589 (the SLIM LIVER Study), which investigated semaglutide in NAFLD in the context of metabolic syndrome, insulin resistance, elevated hepatic lipids, and cardiovascular risk; and a Phase 4 study (NCT05813249) titled 'Semaglutide in Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease,' which has also completed. Two studies have an unknown current status: NCT05779644, a not-applicable-phase trial examining GLP-1 receptor agonist effects on body weight, metabolism, and fat distribution in overweight/obese patients with type 2 diabetes; and NCT06005012, a Phase 2 real-world study of semaglutide for NAFLD-related fibrosis in obesity and type 2 diabetes. Two non-pivotal Phase 2 trials remain active: NCT03884075 (NAFLD HEROES), which is actively recruiting and examines the hepatic response to oral glucose and the effect of semaglutide in NAFLD; and NCT05067621, which is active but no longer recruiting and evaluates semaglutide effects in obese youth with prediabetes or new-onset type 2 diabetes and metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease.
