## Findings by Disease

### Raynaud Disease

#### Literature — Raynaud Disease

**Evidence strength:** strong, mixed
**Relevant studies:** 4

Multiple randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials have evaluated sildenafil for Raynaud's phenomenon (RP), yielding mixed results. Modified-release sildenafil significantly reduced RP attack frequency in patients with limited cutaneous systemic sclerosis (PMID: 21360507), and sildenafil 100 mg/day improved digital blood flow and RP symptoms in systemic sclerosis patients over 8 weeks (PMID: 28281457). However, on-demand sildenafil showed only a high probability of superiority over placebo without clinically relevant aggregated effect sizes, though with substantial inter-patient heterogeneity (PMID: 30383134), and a mechanistic study found that sildenafil increases sympathetically mediated vascular tone via baroreflex activation, which may partially offset its vasodilatory effects (PMID: 23443723).

**Key findings:**
- Modified-release sildenafil reduced mean percentage of RP attacks per week by -44.0% versus -18.1% for placebo (P=0.034) in patients with RP secondary to limited cutaneous systemic sclerosis (PMID: 21360507).
- Sildenafil 100 mg/day for 8 weeks significantly improved finger blood flow before and after cold stimulus (p=0.026 and p=0.028, respectively) and reduced RP duration and VAS severity score compared to placebo in systemic sclerosis patients (PMID: 28281457).
- On-demand sildenafil at 40 mg or 80 mg had greater than 90% probability of being superior to placebo for most outcomes, but aggregated effect sizes were not clinically relevant and substantial heterogeneity in response was observed among patients (PMID: 30383134).
- Sildenafil 100 mg significantly increased plasma norepinephrine and increased sympathetically mediated vascular tone (phentolamine-induced forearm vascular resistance reduction was significantly lower after sildenafil than placebo: -63% ± 3% vs -73% ± 3%; P=0.0002), suggesting alpha-adrenergic vasoconstriction may offset vasodilation (PMID: 23443723).
- Secondary endpoints including Raynaud's Condition Score, duration of attacks, and RP pain score did not differ significantly between modified-release sildenafil and placebo groups (PMID: 21360507).

### Covid-19

#### Literature — Covid-19

**Evidence strength:** moderate, mixed
**Relevant studies:** 2

Two randomized, placebo-controlled trials evaluated sildenafil in COVID-19 patients and both reported clinically meaningful benefits. In severe COVID-19 patients with pulmonary arterial hypertension, sildenafil significantly reduced mortality and intubation risks when adjusted for PAH (OR=0.21 and OR=0.26, respectively), and shortened ICU stay (15 vs. 19 days, p<0.001) (PMID: 37243243). In COVID-19 inpatients with perfusion mismatch on CT angiography, sildenafil was associated with no requirement for invasive mechanical ventilation (vs. 4 patients in placebo, p=0.04) and a shorter hospital stay (9 vs. 12 days, p=0.04), though primary oxygenation endpoints were not significantly different (PMID: 34980198). The evidence supports repurposing sildenafil as an add-on therapy for specific subgroups of COVID-19 patients, though both trials were small and larger confirmatory studies are needed.

**Key findings:**
- In a double-blind RCT of 150 ICU patients with severe COVID-19 and PAH, sildenafil (0.25 mg/kg t.i.d. for one week) significantly reduced mortality risk (OR=0.21, 95% CI: 0.05-0.89) and intubation risk (OR=0.26, 95% CI: 0.08-0.86) when adjusted for PAH, compared to placebo (PMID: 37243243).
- Sildenafil treatment significantly shortened ICU stay compared to placebo (15 vs. 19 days, p<0.001) in severe COVID-19 patients with PAH (PMID: 37243243).
- In a triple-blinded RCT of 40 COVID-19 inpatients with perfusion mismatch on subtraction CT angiography, no patients in the sildenafil arm required invasive mechanical ventilation compared to 4 patients in the placebo arm (logrank p=0.04) (PMID: 34980198).
- Sildenafil-treated COVID-19 patients with perfusion mismatch had a significantly shorter median hospital stay than placebo-treated patients (9 IQR 7-12 days vs. 12 IQR 9-21 days, p=0.04) (PMID: 34980198).
- No statistically significant differences in primary oxygenation parameters (PaO2/FiO2 ratio and A-a gradient) were found between sildenafil and placebo groups in COVID-19 patients with perfusion mismatch (repeated-measures ANOVA p=0.67 and p=0.69) (PMID: 34980198).

### Hypertension

#### Literature — Hypertension

**Evidence strength:** moderate, mixed
**Relevant studies:** 3

Evidence for sildenafil in treating hypertension is mixed. A randomized, double-blind crossover study found that regular sildenafil (50 mg three times daily for 16 days) significantly reduced ambulatory and clinic blood pressure in untreated hypertensive subjects compared to placebo, supporting its potential as antihypertensive therapy (PMID: 16940217). However, a post-hoc subanalysis of five placebo-controlled trials found that sildenafil produced only small, clinically non-significant reductions in blood pressure in men with erectile dysfunction taking antihypertensive medication (PMID: 11132612), and a controlled crossover study in healthy men showed sildenafil increased sympathetically mediated vascular tone and plasma norepinephrine, suggesting a counteracting vasoconstrictor mechanism that may limit its antihypertensive effect (PMID: 23443723).

**Key findings:**
- In a randomized, double-blind, 2-way crossover study of 22 untreated hypertensive subjects, sildenafil 50 mg three times daily for 16 days reduced average daytime ambulatory systolic BP by 8 mmHg versus an increase of 2 mmHg with placebo (P<0.01) and diastolic BP by 6 mmHg versus 0 mmHg with placebo (P<0.01) (PMID: 16940217)
- Sildenafil also reduced clinic systolic BP by 5 mmHg versus an increase of 4 mmHg with placebo (P<0.01) and clinic diastolic BP by 5 mmHg versus an increase of 2 mmHg with placebo (P<0.01) in hypertensive subjects (PMID: 16940217)
- Sildenafil reduced arterial wave reflection both acutely and after chronic treatment in hypertensive subjects, though the chronic change was not statistically different from placebo (PMID: 16940217)
- In a post-hoc subanalysis of five randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials, mean changes from baseline in systolic/diastolic BP for men taking antihypertensive medication were only -3.6/-1.9 mmHg for sildenafil versus -0.8/-0.1 mmHg for placebo, deemed clinically non-significant (PMID: 11132612)
- In a randomized, double-blind crossover study of 9 healthy middle-aged men, sildenafil 100 mg significantly increased plasma norepinephrine compared with placebo at 60 minutes post-administration (P=0.02) (PMID: 23443723)
- The percentage reduction in forearm vascular resistance during phentolamine (an alpha-blocker) was significantly lower after sildenafil than placebo (-63% ± 3% vs -73% ± 3%; P=0.0002), indicating that sildenafil increased sympathetically mediated vascular tone that could counteract its vasodilatory effects (PMID: 23443723)
