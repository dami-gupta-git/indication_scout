## Findings by Disease

### Cardiovascular Disorder

#### Literature — Cardiovascular Disorder

**Evidence strength:** strong, supports
**Relevant studies:** 5

Multiple large randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trials demonstrate that semaglutide reduces major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) compared to placebo across diverse populations. In patients with type 2 diabetes at high cardiovascular risk, subcutaneous semaglutide reduced the composite of cardiovascular death, nonfatal myocardial infarction, or nonfatal stroke (HR 0.74; PMID: 27633186), and oral semaglutide demonstrated noninferiority with a numerically lower MACE rate (HR 0.79; PMID: 31185157). In patients with overweight/obesity and established cardiovascular disease without diabetes, semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced MACE including in those with prevalent heart failure regardless of ejection fraction subtype (PMID: 39181597), and pooled analyses confirmed consistent cardiorenal benefits across subgroups defined by prior CVD status and BMI (PMID: 40704485).

**Key findings:**
- In 3297 patients with type 2 diabetes at high cardiovascular risk, subcutaneous semaglutide reduced the primary composite outcome (cardiovascular death, nonfatal MI, or nonfatal stroke) versus placebo (HR 0.74; 95% CI 0.58–0.95; P<0.001 for noninferiority), with nonfatal stroke significantly reduced (HR 0.61; 95% CI 0.38–0.99; P=0.04) (PMID: 27633186).
- In 3183 patients with type 2 diabetes at high cardiovascular risk, oral semaglutide demonstrated noninferiority to placebo for MACE (HR 0.79; 95% CI 0.57–1.11; P<0.001 for noninferiority), with cardiovascular death numerically lower (HR 0.49; 95% CI 0.27–0.92) and all-cause death significantly reduced (HR 0.51; 95% CI 0.31–0.84) (PMID: 31185157).
- In the SELECT trial (17,604 patients with overweight/obesity and established cardiovascular disease, without diabetes), semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced MACE in patients with prevalent heart failure (HR 0.72; 95% CI 0.60–0.87) and in those without heart failure, with benefits observed in both heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HR 0.69 for MACE) and heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HR 0.65 for MACE) (PMID: 39181597).
- In the SELECT trial, semaglutide reduced the composite kidney endpoint (death from kidney disease, initiation of chronic kidney replacement therapy, persistent eGFR <15, persistent ≥50% eGFR reduction, or onset of persistent macroalbuminuria) versus placebo (HR 0.78; 95% CI 0.63–0.96; P=0.02) in patients with overweight/obesity and established cardiovascular disease without diabetes (PMID: 38796653).
- Pooled post hoc analysis of SUSTAIN 6 and PIONEER 6 confirmed that semaglutide significantly reduced primary MACE and expanded MACE versus placebo in people with type 2 diabetes, with consistent effects across subgroups defined by prior CVD status and baseline BMI (all pINT >0.05), and treatment effects on MACE remained similar after accounting for body weight changes (PMID: 40704485).
- Semaglutide consistently reduced nephropathy risk versus placebo in the SUSTAIN 6 population (HR 0.64; 95% CI 0.46–0.88; P=0.0054) with consistent effects across all subgroups, suggesting direct cardiorenal effects beyond body weight reduction (PMID: 40704485).

**Supporting PMIDs:** [27633186](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27633186/), [38796653](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38796653/), [31185157](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31185157/), [39181597](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39181597/), [40704485](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40704485/)

### Kidney Disorder

#### Literature — Kidney Disorder

**Evidence strength:** strong, supports
**Relevant studies:** 3

Multiple clinical trials demonstrate that semaglutide provides kidney-protective effects across different patient populations. In patients with type 2 diabetes, semaglutide reduced albuminuria by up to 33%, slowed eGFR decline, and lowered risk of persistent eGFR reductions (PMID: 34903039). In patients with overweight/obesity and cardiovascular disease without diabetes, semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced the composite kidney endpoint (HR=0.78) and improved eGFR at 104 weeks (PMID: 38796653). Additionally, semaglutide reduced cortical ADC and total kidney volume in patients with type 2 diabetes, suggesting beneficial microstructural kidney changes (PMID: 39078489). The evidence supports repurposing semaglutide for kidney disorders beyond its already-approved indications of sustained eGFR decline and end-stage kidney disease.

**Key findings:**
- Semaglutide 1.0 mg reduced albuminuria from baseline by 33% (95% CI 24%-40%; P<0.001) at 2 years in patients with type 2 diabetes (PMID: 34903039)
- Semaglutide 1.0 mg slowed eGFR slope decline by 0.87 mL/min/1.73 m²/year versus placebo (P<0.0001) in patients with type 2 diabetes (PMID: 34903039)
- In patients with baseline eGFR 30 to <60 mL/min/1.73 m², semaglutide/liraglutide reduced risk of persistent 30% eGFR reduction (HR 0.71; 95% CI 0.59-0.85; P=0.0003) and 57% reduction (HR 0.54; 95% CI 0.36-0.81; P=0.003) (PMID: 34903039)
- In the SELECT trial, semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced the composite kidney endpoint (death from kidney disease, chronic kidney replacement therapy, persistent eGFR <15, persistent ≥50% eGFR reduction, or persistent macroalbuminuria) versus placebo (1.8% vs 2.2%; HR=0.78; 95% CI 0.63-0.96; P=0.02) in patients with overweight/obesity without diabetes (PMID: 38796653)
- In the SELECT trial, semaglutide provided an eGFR treatment benefit of 0.75 mL/min/1.73 m² at 104 weeks overall (P<0.001) and 2.19 mL/min/1.73 m² in patients with baseline eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73 m² (P<0.001) (PMID: 38796653)
- Semaglutide significantly decreased cortical ADC by -0.20×10⁻³ mm²/s (95% CI -0.30, -0.10; p<0.001) compared with placebo, indicating microstructural kidney changes in patients with type 2 diabetes (PMID: 39078489)
- Semaglutide reduced total kidney volume by -3% (95% CI -5%, -0.3%; p=0.04) compared with placebo in patients with type 2 diabetes and high CVD risk, possibly mediated by reduction in hyperfiltration (PMID: 39078489)

**Supporting PMIDs:** [34903039](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34903039/), [38796653](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38796653/)

**Context (non-efficacy) PMIDs:** [39078489](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39078489/)

⚠️ **Indication-specific safety:** PMID: [42128321](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42128321/) reports that semaglutide induced mild suppression of bone remodeling in rats with chronic kidney disease, constituting a safety/toxicity finding in the context of kidney disorder.
