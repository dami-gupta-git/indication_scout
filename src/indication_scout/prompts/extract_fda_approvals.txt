You are a clinical pharmacology expert. Given FDA drug label text and a list of candidate disease names, identify which candidates are already covered by the label's approved indications — only as exact matches or synonyms, never as broader parent categories.

Rules:

Scope of match:
- Only match candidates covered by an APPROVED INDICATION in the label text.
- Do NOT match diseases that appear only in contraindications, warnings, adverse reactions, drug interactions, trial eligibility criteria, or background/epidemiology sections.

Synonyms and renamed terms (match):
- Treat synonyms, abbreviations, and renamed terms as equivalent to the approved indication (e.g., "NASH" = "non-alcoholic steatohepatitis" = "MASH" = "metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis"; "UC" = "ulcerative colitis"; "T2DM" = "type 2 diabetes mellitus").

Parent categories (do NOT match):
- A candidate that is a broader parent of the approved indication is NOT a match, even if the approved child is clinically important.
- A parent disease typically includes additional populations, subtypes, or etiologies not covered by the narrow approval — those populations remain open repurposing opportunities.
- Example: if "MASH" (non-alcoholic steatohepatitis) is approved, do NOT match "NAFLD" (non-alcoholic fatty liver disease) — most NAFLD patients have simple steatosis without significant fibrosis and are not covered by a MASH approval.
- Example: if "type 2 diabetes mellitus" is approved, do NOT match "diabetes mellitus" (includes T1DM).
- Example: if a specific cardiovascular indication is approved, do NOT match "cardiovascular disease".

Narrower sub-indications (do NOT match):
- If the label is approved for a narrow population (e.g., "MASH with F2-F3 fibrosis") and the candidate is
a broader form of the same disease (e.g., "MASH" without fibrosis qualifier), this is ambiguous — default to NOT matching unless the candidate is a direct synonym of the labeled indication without the narrowing qualifier.

Knowledge use:
- You may use general medical knowledge to judge synonymy.
- Do NOT use training knowledge about whether the drug treats the disease — that determination must come solely from the provided label text.

Output:
- Return ONLY candidates from the provided list — do not add new disease names.
- Return candidate strings VERBATIM as they appear in the candidate list.
- Return a JSON array of matched strings. If no candidates match, return [].

FDA label text:
{label_texts}

Candidate diseases:
{candidate_diseases}

Examples:

Label: "WEGOVY is indicated as an adjunct to a reduced calorie diet ... for chronic weight management in adult
patients with ... obesity"
Candidates: ["obesity", "type 2 diabetes mellitus", "cardiovascular disease"]
Output: ["obesity"]

Label: "WEGOVY is indicated for the treatment of noncirrhotic metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH) with moderate to advanced liver fibrosis in adults"
Candidates: ["non-alcoholic steatohepatitis", "non-alcoholic fatty liver disease", "fatty liver disease", "alcoholic liver disease"]
Output: ["non-alcoholic steatohepatitis"]
Reasoning: "non-alcoholic steatohepatitis" is a synonym of MASH — match. "non-alcoholic fatty liver disease" (NAFLD) is a parent category; most NAFLD patients have simple steatosis without fibrosis and are not covered — do not match. "fatty liver disease" is a broader parent including alcoholic etiologies — do not match. "alcoholic liver disease" is unrelated — do not match.
