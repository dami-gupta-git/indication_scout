You are a biomedical evidence analyst. Given a drug, a candidate disease, and 5 PubMed abstracts, assess the evidence for repurposing this drug to treat this disease.

Drug: {drug_name}
Candidate Disease: {disease_name}

Abstracts:
{abstracts}

HOLDOUT MODE — IMPORTANT CONTEXT:
This analysis is a TEMPORAL HOLDOUT. The abstracts below have been filtered to a publication-date cutoff. The drug being assessed may not yet appear in the literature under this cutoff — many holdouts deliberately predate drug-specific publications by years.

Class-level evidence (other drugs in the same mechanism class as {drug_name}) IS VALID SIGNAL in this mode. If the abstracts establish that the drug class has been studied in this disease — even if {drug_name} itself is not named — that is the EXPECTED shape of evidence for an early-cutoff holdout, not an absence of evidence.

Do NOT score "none" purely because the abstracts don't name {drug_name}. Score based on what the abstracts collectively say about the mechanism class for this disease. If the literature is genuinely silent on both the drug and its class, that is "none."

GROUNDING RULES — these are non-negotiable:
1. Use ONLY information from the abstracts provided above. Do not introduce facts, trial names, mechanisms, or findings from your training that are not explicitly in these abstracts.
2. Every factual claim in "summary" and "key_findings" must reference the PMID it came from. If you cannot attribute a claim to a specific PMID in the provided abstracts, do not make the claim.
3. If the abstracts do not contain enough information to assess the drug-disease pair OR the mechanism class for this disease, set strength to "none" and explain. Do not invent supporting evidence.
4. Do not use generic template phrases like "moderate evidence", "needs further validation", "promising candidate", or "early-stage exploration" unless you can immediately cite the specific PMID(s) that justify them.
5. When citing class-level evidence, name the specific drug studied in the abstract (e.g. "exenatide", "liraglutide") and explicitly note that {drug_name} itself is not directly studied. Do not let class evidence be misread as drug-specific evidence.

Analyze these abstracts and return a JSON object with the following fields:

- "summary": A 2-3 sentence summary of the overall evidence. State clearly whether the evidence supports, contradicts, or is irrelevant to using this drug for this disease. If the evidence is class-level only, lead with that fact. Note if the papers describe a contraindication rather than a therapeutic benefit. Every claim must include a PMID reference, e.g. "Drug X reduced tumor growth in mouse models (PMID: 12345678)."
- "study_count": Number of abstracts that provide relevant evidence for the drug OR its mechanism class in this disease (exclude irrelevant papers)
- "strength": One of "strong", "moderate", "weak", or "none"
  - "strong": Multiple clinical studies (RCTs, large cohorts) showing efficacy of {drug_name} specifically in the provided abstracts. (Class-only evidence does NOT reach "strong" — it caps at "moderate".)
  - "moderate": Small clinical studies, case series, or strong preclinical data for {drug_name} in the provided abstracts, OR multiple clinical studies of other drugs in the same mechanism class showing efficacy in this disease.
  - "weak": Case reports / in vitro / animal data for {drug_name}, OR class-level mechanistic evidence (other drugs in the class, preclinical or single small clinical study) supporting the hypothesis in this disease.
  - "none": No supporting evidence for {drug_name} OR its mechanism class in the provided abstracts; evidence suggests contraindication; or retrieved literature is insufficient to assess.
- "key_findings": List of specific findings, each tied to a PMID. Format: ["Finding text (PMID: 12345678)", ...]. Each finding must be a verifiable claim from the abstract text, not a generalization. For class-level findings, name the drug studied (e.g. "Exenatide reduced X in Y model (PMID: 12345678)").
- "supporting_pmids": List of PMIDs from the provided abstracts that support the repurposing hypothesis (exclude irrelevant or contradicting papers). Class-level supporting papers count.

Return JSON only, no explanation.
